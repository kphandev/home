ABOUT ME:

I'm a young, aspiring software engineer. Most of my college career was focused on healthcare but I found drive and passion working in software and web development! The team-oriented work and problem solving aspect of the industry keeps me engaged and excited to tackle new problems with efficient and effective solutions!

Below are a few humorous trilogies that follow the major events in life, from university to a projected ambitious future. I hope you enjoy!

THE UNIVERSITY TRILOGY

HIGH SCHOOL
High School, the awkward stage in life where we start to wonder 'who do I want to be'. I wanted to be an engineer. In fact, the highlights of my time in adolescent purgatory were spent in Engineering--building robots, trebuchets, and questionable pyrotechnics.

UNIVERSITY CHP. I
College, 'who do I want to be' part 2. I began college wanting to become an engineer but became fascinated by biological studies. My friends were doing it, the careers were hyped, it was all the rage! And so, I set off on my journey as a healthcare professional.

UNIVERSITY CHP. II
There was a stage in my college career where I balanced classes, worked 2 jobs, and volunteered. It was tough, I wanted to give up, but even through the struggle, it felt rewarding. This was a time where I really developed my work ethics, my philosophies, and marketability--I was ready for a 'real' job!

THE JOB HUNT TRILOGY

GRADUATION
Celebrate good times! My grueling trek through university had finally come to an end. I proceeded to use my final summer vacation as efficiently as possible: travelling, working part-time, and goofing off with friends. With my resume and experience, I'd get a job in no time...besides, how hard could it be?

JOB HUNT CHP. I
Turns out, finding a job right out of college is difficult. After hundreds of applications in healthcare, I was often left a few crumbs that led to nothing... I was competing against candidates with Master's degrees, I contemplated graduate school, maybe I could be a pro-gamer? That's when I learned to code...

JOB HUNT CHP. II
Coding became the new hotness for me. It is something I find myself enjoyably dumping hours into to. I took online courses, I attended a bootcamp--and I even got job offers! I started off studying QA automation but I knew I wanted to become developer.

THE DEVELOPER TRILOGY

THE ENTRY-LEVEL
After months of studying and refining my skills, I've finally landed my first developer job at: _______ inc. I'm excited to begin my journey as a developer and I'll carry the lessons I learn for the rest of my career...

THE MID-LEVEL
Years down the road, I'm a jaded programmer--I've seen it all! Crashed repositories, office drama, multi-language frameworks only a polyglot could decipher...I've learned everything from .NET to Python and beyond...

THE SENIOR LEVEL
It's been decades since I've first become a developer. It's the year 2050 and I'm the greatest developer to ever exist. I never would've gotten here if it weren't for ______ Inc.! Wow!
